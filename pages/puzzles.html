Nyansapo

Rowan saw an answer box and an image below with a riddle at the top. “At the beginning of November that which weighs the least shall lead to Oscar.” The image looked very similar to the graphs they had seen in computer science as it consisted of nodes with labelled connections. Being familiar with algorithms to process graphs, Rowan examined the riddle more closely. Rowan thought that “beginning of November” and “lead to Oscar” meant starting from node N and reaching node O. Rowan interpreted “weighs the least” as a clue that this was a weighted graph and from all the supporting clues this meant that he needed to find the shortest path from N to O. This would lead to the answer that needed to be input. After processing the graph the nodes that lead to O were NYANSAP and made up the word NYANSAPO. Rowan Googled the word as he wasn’t sure what it is or meant and found it was a “wisdom knot”: an African symbol that represented wisdom and more. Seeing that it resembled the graph itself he knew he must have been on the right track. Rowan felt this puzzle was very easy. After inputting this into the answer box, the site loaded an old style map and a button that said “good” on it. Rowan knew there was more behind this than met the eye. At this point, he chose to stop talking about it and followed the rules in the manifesto to keep it secret.

Secret Words

Rowan was presented with an audio file with the following riddle: “Forwards I am heavy, backwards I am not. Fast I am not but slow I should not be. The rise of tone makes me sing.” After downloading it, strange murmurs and distortion were heard. Following the clues, Rowan reversed the audio and speed up the tempo. It took him a while to change the pitch of the audio so that what was heard was made clear. He heard the answer: “2 days from now, at 6pm, be there”. Being that this puzzle was released on the 10th of May and the answer box was a date and time, he input “05/12/2016 6:00 PM” in and was shown another map and a button called “ongoing”. Rowan kept the date in mind as it must mean there was some meeting he must attend but he did not know of the location. The map, which was a portion of the campus map provided by UP to all its students, gave Rowan the impression that this would help him find the location of the meeting place. He compared the first map and the second map given and found they correlated with each other.

Rowan wondered if the figures on the right of the first map which looked like 2 men indicated the location. Rowan kept it in mind.
